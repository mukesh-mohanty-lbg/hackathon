# Ochil Youths Community Improvement (OYCI)

## Project: Systems Improvement

---

## Background of the Charity

* **Website:** [https://www.oyci.org.uk/parents-carers-supporters/#oyci](https://www.oyci.org.uk/parents-carers-supporters/#oyci)
* **Founded:** 2017
* **Location:** Clackmannanshire, Scotland
* **Staff:** 15 members
* **Turnover:** £300,000

### Organisational Context

* **Approach:** Youth-led; children and young people lead the work
* **Mission:** To ensure every young person in the area can flourish
* **Vision:** Universal youth work, with a focus on supporting more marginalised and vulnerable individuals as the charity grows
* **Offerings:** All services are free, including full school holiday programmes with meals provided
* **Policy Role:** Early policy tester for schooling schemes
* **Growth Plan:** Expanding reach to more marginalised and vulnerable young people

---

## Problem

Young people and their families in Clackmannanshire face barriers to accessing OYCI’s services due to manual, fragmented, and sometimes exclusionary processes.

Digital exclusion, complex booking systems, and a lack of integrated data make it harder for the most vulnerable to participate spontaneously or consistently.

### Organisational Challenges

* Key administrative processes (bookings, staff scheduling, data collection) are manual and time-consuming
* No central CRM; data fragmented across:

  * Bookeo
  * Zoho People
  * Excel
  * MS365
* Bookeo booking system limitations:

  * One account per email
  * Does not reflect real family structures
* Lack of integration:

  * Staff leave in Zoho People does not sync with Excel schedules or Xero payroll
* Manual processes:

  * Limit organisational capacity
  * Create barriers to growth
  * Prevent meaningful data analysis
* Current systems prevent OYCI from scaling its impact and reaching more marginalised young people

## Current State

### What happens now?

* **Bookings:** Managed through Bookeo, but restrictive and not family-friendly
* **Staff scheduling:** Manual, Excel-based, time-consuming and error-prone
* **Staff leave:** Managed in Zoho People, not integrated with scheduling or payroll (Xero)
* **Office tools:** MS365 used, but no central CRM or data system
* **Data collection:** Entirely manual
* **Reporting:** Difficult and time-intensive

### Has anything new been tried?

No. Limited capacity has prevented trials of new digital solutions.

### Stakeholders

* Young people (primary beneficiaries)
* Parents, carers, and families
* OYCI staff (15 members)
* Volunteers
* Community partners
* Funders and local authorities

### Data Availability

Both qualitative and quantitative data are collected, but only manually.
There is no automated or integrated data flow.

### Skills & Capacity

* Limited staff digital skills
* No in-house expertise in systems integration or data management

## Goal

### Desired Outcome

Implement a **simple, user-friendly system** for bookings and data management that:

* Does not exclude digitally disadvantaged families
* Differentiates between:

  * Number of bookings
  * Number of individual young people
  * Frequency of participation
* Makes booking a **supportive tool**, not a barrier
* Integrates:

  * Staff scheduling
  * Leave management
  * Payroll
* Enables spontaneous participation
* Handles annual confidentiality renewals smoothly
* Reduces digital exclusion
* Supports universal access

### Measuring Success

* Reduction in staff admin time
* Increase in spontaneous and repeat participation
* Improved data quality and reporting
* Positive feedback from families and young people
